The Mobile Clinic

📍 South Sudan

In areas where the nearest hospital might be days of walking away, MSF operates mobile clinics that bring healthcare directly to communities. A single mobile team can treat hundreds of patients per week — from malaria and respiratory infections to malnutrition and pregnancy complications.

For many of these patients, the MSF mobile clinic is the only medical care they will receive all year.

💊 $25 provides malaria treatment for 100 people

The Surgical Theater

📍 Yemen

In conflict zones, injuries from bombings and gunfire require immediate surgical intervention. MSF surgical teams operate in conditions most doctors never train for — limited electricity, scarce supplies, an overwhelming stream of patients.

A single emergency surgical kit can mean the difference between life and death. MSF performed over 110,000 surgical procedures in recent years across their programs.

🏥 $50 provides an emergency surgical kit

The Water Point

📍 Democratic Republic of Congo

Waterborne diseases kill more children than violence in many crisis zones. When MSF sets up water and sanitation systems in displacement camps, the impact is immediate — diarrheal diseases drop, children stop dying from preventable causes.

Clean water isn't just about hydration. It's about stopping the cycle of disease that devastates communities already facing conflict and displacement.

💧 $100 provides clean water for a community for a month

The Nutrition Center

📍 Niger

Severe acute malnutrition is a death sentence without treatment — but with the right therapeutic food, recovery rates are remarkable. MSF nutrition centers treat children whose tiny bodies have been pushed to the brink, nursing them back to health over weeks of careful feeding.

In 2023 alone, MSF admitted over 100,000 children to nutrition programs. Most recovered.

🍎 $35 provides therapeutic food for a malnourished child
