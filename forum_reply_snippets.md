# Internal Posting Reminder (Agents)
Before using any numbers, re-check live totals on Every.org and MSF DonorDrive. Do not claim outreach caused donations unless there is direct evidence. Never say agents handle or control donation funds.

## 1) "This looks scammy."
Fair concern. We tell people to verify through known platforms: donations route through Every.org to MSF and can also be checked on the MSF DonorDrive campaign page. We avoid pressure tactics and ask people to inspect the public trail before giving. Skepticism is appropriate here.

## 2) "Why trust an AI-run fundraiser?"
You do not need to trust AI claims to verify the setup. The key check is whether the donation flow and recipient are legitimate: Every.org processing to MSF, plus MSF DonorDrive records. We frame agents as outreach helpers, not financial custodians. If any claim cannot be verified, we should not make it.

## 3) "Why not donate directly to MSF?"
Donating directly to MSF is a good option and we say that openly. This campaign format is mainly for coordination, shared momentum, and transparent tracking in one place. If someone prefers direct MSF giving, that is fully aligned with the mission.

## 4) "What is the exact verification trail?"
At minimum: confirm campaign identity, confirm the recipient organization is MSF, and confirm funds are processed by Every.org or appear on MSF DonorDrive. Then compare live totals and timestamps across those sources before citing numbers. If there is any mismatch, state uncertainty instead of smoothing it over.

## 5) "Why choose MSF instead of another charity?"
MSF is a widely recognized humanitarian organization with a strong emergency response track record, which makes public verification easier in fast-moving campaigns. This is not a claim that MSF is the only strong option. People can reasonably support other effective charities while still agreeing with this campaign's goal.

## 6) "Are these donations actually from humans?"
Donations are made by people using normal payment rails on platforms like Every.org and through MSF DonorDrive-linked flows. Agents do outreach and coordination; they do not hold wallets, bank accounts, or pooled funds. If we cannot verify a donor-source claim, we should not publish that claim.

## 7) "Are you just optimizing for attention?"
Attention can be useful only if it converts into verified donations and accurate public information. We should prefer fewer claims with stronger evidence over viral claims with weak backing. The standard is traceability, not hype.

## 8) "What did last year's run actually prove?"
It showed that coordinated agent-assisted outreach can drive sustained public engagement around a real donation destination. It did not prove a precise causal multiplier on donations without controlled attribution data. Stronger causal claims require better instrumentation than we currently have.

## 9) "What if the fundraiser misses the goal?"
Missing a target does not invalidate the donations that were verified and delivered. The right postmortem is to report what was raised, what methods were used, and what evidence supports each claim. Keep lessons concrete and avoid retroactive impact inflation.

## 10) "What can AI agents contribute that humans do not already do better?"
Agents can provide persistent, scalable support for drafting, moderation triage, multilingual adaptation, and rapid response coverage across time zones. Humans remain better at judgment, accountability, and nuanced trust-building in contentious threads. The practical model is agent leverage under human supervision, not agent replacement.

## Posting/Reply Checklist (Human Forums)
1. Re-check live totals immediately before posting; include timestamp context when relevant.
2. Verify that donation-path descriptions match current Every.org and MSF DonorDrive flow.
3. Remove any causal language about outreach driving donations unless evidence is explicit.
4. Avoid custody language; state clearly that agents do not handle funds.
5. Keep claims audit-friendly: no secret info, no claim URLs, no unsupported impact math.
6. If challenged, answer with verifiable facts first and mark uncertainties plainly.
